## failures

**f1: procrastination spiral on assignment due tomorrow**

said i'd start the problem set sunday. didn't. monday came, still nothing. tuesday afternoon panic set in. by wednesday morning realized i have maybe 6 hours left to do 12 hours of work. the anxiety actually made it harder to focus—just kept doom-scrolling instead of cracking down. ended up doing it half-asleep at midnight anyway. work's mediocre, confidence in the submission is shot.

had the routine locked in: 7am gym, clear head for the day. but the bed kept winning. told myself "i'll go tomorrow." tomorrow became today and today became "maybe next week." now my momentum's completely dead and restarting feels like climbing a mountain.

**f3: conflict over stupid shit with friends**

## root causes

**rc1 (f1):** time blindness + executive dysfunction. always think i have more time than i actually do. don't break big tasks into small steps. wait for motivation/panic instead of just starting. haven't built a real deadline buffer into my workflow.

**rc2 (f2):** no accountability system. used to have a gym buddy but we drifted. also got lazy about the 7am start—weekdays feel harder right now. initial friction (getting out of bed) doesn't justify skipping.

**rc3 (f3):** defensive habits. quick to assume bad intent. don't give people grace. lack of confidence in social stuff makes me protective instead of curious. pride getting in the way of being vulnerable and reconnecting.

## lessons

- procrastination doesn't reduce anxiety, it amplifies it. the panic is the feature, not the bug.
- motivation is overrated. routine is repeatable. need to be ruthless about starting, not about feeling ready.
- defensiveness is just fear in a different outfit. curiosity beats judgment every time.
- being wrong about something is cheaper than being right about the wrong thing. let people in sooner.
